2. Simulation Results Are Estimates

The battle simulation engine produces mathematical estimates based on a reverse-engineered combat model. These estimates:

  • May differ from actual in-game battle outcomes by 15–30% or more.
  • Do not account for all in-game variables, including but not limited to: hero abilities, buffs from active events, rally mechanics, server lag, or special skills.
  • Are intended to rank formations relatively — the top-ranked formation is likely (but not guaranteed) to outperform lower-ranked ones.
  • Are not suitable for making high-stakes decisions without cross-referencing with real battle reports.

3. Formation Rankings vs Absolute Numbers

The relative ranking of formations (e.g., Formation A performs better than Formation B) is well-validated against real battle data. The absolute injury numbers shown are estimates and should be treated as directional guides, not exact predictions.
